IKWETA Safari Camp Nature Trails Cost: Free

Take a walk along the nature trails to stretch the legs, especially after a long drive. Approximately 10-15 minutes. Morning or evening.

Nyambene Forest and Water Tower Cost: KES 3,000 for entry into the forest & guide

The excursion takes visitors through scenic landscapes where some of the best quality tea in the world is grown. It culminates in the visit to Nyambene forest and water tower and includes a tour of the sources and waterworks of the Tuuru Water Scheme, an endevour of the Catholic Diocese of Meru and the local communities. The Tuuru Water Scheme distributes water over an area of 1,200 sq. KM of both low and high population densities. The aqueducts extend over 250 km and channel over 36 million liters of water per day to more than a quarter million people and more than 60,000 large and small domestic animals. Half-day and full-day options may include packed lunch in Nyambene Forest (by arrangement).

Magado [Igombe] Crater – Nyambene National Reserve Cost: KES 3,000 Full-day Excursion

This excursion takes visitor to the Magado Crater [also known as Igombe among the Meru] located within the Nyambene National Reserve. The crater is a spectacular site with dark purple, bright red and green pools. A lake on the floor of the crater evaporates to form soda salt crust. Meru people have been collecting soda in this crater since time immemorial. More recently, Borana and Somali people have been sharing the crater. The local people water their cattle in this natural collecting basin. During the dry season as the full lake recedes, the soda that remains behind is collected.
